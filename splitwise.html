Splitwise

Mobile App Experience Redesign

OVERVIEW

Touching up blemishes
on Splitwise

Did you ever split bills with your friends for a party or a trip? You are not alone, especially if you are living in the city. Now modern technology aims to help people in every way possible. Split bill apps make sharing bills easier and faster.


I have been using Splitwise for a recurring expense with my friends. Without using a calculator, it successfully solved the problem with equal or unequal splits. However, despite using it religiously, I have trouble navigating through it. It made me curious as to if the other users also experienced issues with the app and the issues they were facing. I pursued this redesign as an opportunity to evolve the experience in any way I could.

WHAT I FOUND

Current problems

Visual Clutter

Too many elements without hierarchy are all competing for a user’s attention and add effort to the user’s journey.

Unintuitive Navigation

It is not organized and hard to navigate. The complicated journey makes users get lost in the app.

WHAT I AM GOING TO DO

Solutions

Effective Visual Hierarchy

Declutter the pages and structured copy content have a high level of legibility, so users can scan it quickly and they don't need to make a effort to distinguish the data they’ve been looking for.

Optimize the Journey

Remove all friction by making navigation simple and intuitive. Understanding customer behaviors enables to redesign the journey based on proven facts, with customer needs in mind.

MY GOAL

Stand out to customers
and achieve brand loyalty

RETHINK SPLITWISE

My design process

Throughout the process, I was constantly iterating and conducting Usability Testing to ensure that the ideas I came up with are being validated.

RESEARCH

Dig deep its users and competitors

I started with a brand analysis of Splitwise, learning about its competitors and users' habits along the way.

App Store Reviews

To complement the user interview, I wanted to look through some of Splitwise’s most critical App Store reviews in the past year or so. Users noted that they were frustrated and the app is not user friendly.

Online Survey

I conducted an online survey to further understand user motivation and behavior. The survey asked respondents questions about their finance and spliting bill habits. The users fucus on the main features, they are not interested in secondary features or even users were not aware of them.

Competitor Analysis

To know the strengths and weaknesses of your competition, I collected and compared data about the popular splitting bills apps.

OBSERVATIONS

Make better decisions
based on valid insights and facts

I was curious to find out users' experience that provides the accurate information about people, their tasks, and their needs.

User Testing

I observed to participants and recorded their actions as they attempt to complete tasks with the existing Splitwise interface without interrupting them or affecting their behavior. Some of the tasks/questions were:


  • Tasks to perform include: add a bill, settle up the bills,check the history.

  • Could they not make it through the task?

  • Did they make it through the task but hit some errors along the way?

  • How long did it take to complete the task?

  • Participants evaluate what they most like and dislike about the experience.

This is what users have said during the usability tests:

  • “Uhh…don’t know”

  • "How do I go back?"

  • "It's done?"

Testing

According on the scenarios, I conducted two rounds of user testing with 6 participants in each round. I gave my participants my low-fidelity wireframes and had them complete the three tasks. I then recorded their comments and went back to my wireframes to make changes.

Based on the feedback I received, I created medium-fideltiy wireframes and presented the participants in the second round of testing. I then tracked down their feedback and consolidated my findings into three main points.

1

The whole operation is smooth.

2

It’s unnoticeable and unclickable that navigation is at the top of the page.

3

The title bar doesn’t show a progression.

FINAL DESIGN

Discovering Splitwise

Starting with
the welcome screens

Shaping the story of Splitwise before the user first time sign in on the new device is the easiest way to promote the product to get more customers.

Design Problem 1

How to delight the users?

Solution

I designed and applied a serious of mascots for the brand to imitate the flow of real communication with the user and set emotional bonds.


The friendly interface with a powerful copy helps the users get oriented within unfamiliar features and controls as well as understand if Splitwise can be useful for them.

Optimizing the journey of adding a bill

Adding the expense is the core task of Splitwise, users want to feel they can do all steps simply and quickly.

Design Problem 2

How to be clear and concise?

Solution

To simplify - visual hierarchy plays a key role to help users navigate through the app easier, and can drastically reduce the amount of effort needed to engage with the app.


Visibility - I designed the progress tracker that guides the user through a number of steps in order to complete the task.

Get to know own balance statement

After research, the transaction history and the balance statement are the main parts that users want to know about personal finance.

Design Problem 3

How to define user’s needs statement？

Solution

As far as users are concerned, they are more curious about personal balance statement and expense activities. So I merged "Activity" into "Me", it shows the personal balance clearly, and it is fast to check the transaction history.


Reviewing the research, I noticed a lack of knowledge with checking the expense history over a specific period of time. To bring more organization for the users, I added the filter button to guide users to easily access their monthly transactions.

East and fast to have a new group

Building a group quickly makes other users to add the bill as early as possible.
